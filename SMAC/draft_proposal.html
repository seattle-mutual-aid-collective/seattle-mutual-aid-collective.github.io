SEATTLE MUTUAL AID COLLECTIVE

SMAC-Hub: Emergency Communications and Resilience Hub

The City of Seattle recognizes the need for community-led resilience initiatives that strengthen connections between neighbors and communities. The Seattle Office of Emergency Management and the Department of Neighborhoods support active development of new projects establishing Community Emergency Hubs for short-term crisis response and Resilience Hubs for long-term organization of community wellbeing.

The Seattle Mutual Aid Collective is a community organization working on enhancing local resilience efforts and strengthening support networks for vulnerable community members. We are a queer and trans-operated group focusing on delivering mutual aid connections and resources to socially vulnerable individuals who face barriers in accessing neighborhood resilience resources due to geographic, social, or economic isolation.

Project Description

This proposal seeks funding from the Neighborhood Matching Fund for a comprehensive Emergency Communications and Resilience Workshop Series, with practical skills, training and resources that will be put to concrete use through the establishment of complementary Community Emergency Communications and Resilience Hubs.

Our project addresses a critical community need by creating resilience pathways both within neighborhoods and across Seattle's diverse communities. By strengthening connections between isolated individuals and established neighborhood structures, we build a more robust citywide emergency response network that leaves no one behind.

We are supported by community interest and engagement, with 10 volunteers pledging their time and expertise to this proposal. These community members bring diverse skills in emergency preparedness, communications technology, and community organizing.

Seattle Mutual Aid Collective Resilience Workshop Series

Our core programming consists of a workshop series designed to build practical skills and distribute essential resources:

Emergency First Aid Training

  • First aid training with the Office of Emergency Management's Stop-the-Bleed program
  • Distribution of essential first aid supplies to underserved neighbors

Emergency Preparedness Workshops

  • Emergency kit and go-bag workshop with Pipsqueak Community Space
  • Community emergency planning and household resiliency preparation
  • Distribution of emergency supply kits and go-bag supplies to individuals and households with limited resources

Communications Resilience Training

  • Radio training workshop with Punks in the Park
  • Distribution of handheld battery-operated radios to enhance community preparedness
  • "Power Down Field Exercise": Coordination with Seattle Emergency Communication Hubs and the Auxiliary Communication Service to practice communications when standard systems fail

Climate Adaptation and Food Security

  • Climate change reality, resilience and adaptation workshop with Oceanography PhD, Dr. Mora Groussman
  • Community gardening workshop by SMAC Seed Collective
  • Promotion of hyperlocal food security solutions
  • Contribution to diversity of local seed bank

All workshops will include targeted outreach to vulnerable and underserved communities who often face challenges accessing standard neighborhood-based resilience efforts.

Establishment of Community Emergency Communications Hub

Building on the workshop series, we will establish a formal Community Emergency Communications Hub in partnership with volunteers from the /dev/hack collective:

  • Registration with the Seattle Emergency Hubs network
  • Training and organization for a core of dedicated volunteers
  • Coordination and practice with the citywide Seattle Emergency Hubs network
  • Creation of bridge connections between individuals and neighborhood-based hubs

Establishment of Community Resilience Hub

Complementing the Communications Hub, we will develop a longer-term Community Resilience Hub:

  • Implementation following City and State guidelines for creating interconnected Resilience Hubs
  • Development of a website featuring publicly accessible educational resources, training materials, and community workshop information in English and Spanish translations
  • Resource directory specifically designed for vulnerable neighbors who may not be connected to traditional neighborhood networks
  • Ongoing support structure to maintain connections between emergency events

By focusing both on emergency communications and long-term resilience, this project creates multiple pathways for community members to engage with emergency preparedness while strengthening the social fabric that supports vulnerable populations before, during, and after crisis events.
