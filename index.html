Radio Access & Volunteer Emergency Networks

Seattle faces escalating climate and disaster risks (earthquakes, wildfires), yet marginalized communities—including BIPOC, LGBTQ+, and low-income residents—are often excluded from preparedness networks. RAVEN (Radio Access & Volunteer Emergency Networks) addresses this gap through a citywide mutual aid initiative led by queer organizers.

We will:

  • Host 12 free workshops (First Aid, Radio Communications, Climate Adaptation) reaching 200+ residents.
  • Distribute 100 emergency radios/kits to vulnerable households.
  • Train volunteers to launch Emergency Communication Hubs, bridging isolated communities with city systems.
A project initiated by the Seattle Mutual Aid Collective in partnership with /dev/hack Collective, RAVEN integrates with Seattle’s Emergency Hub Network while centering hyperlocal needs. By linking mutual aid with citywide infrastructure, RAVEN builds redundancy and equity in disaster response.
