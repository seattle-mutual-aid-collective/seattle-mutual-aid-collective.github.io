Community Networks

Community Benefit

Seattle faces escalating climate and seismic risks that disproportionately impact vulnerable populations. Our project addresses documented gaps in disaster preparedness infrastructure:

Nearly 50% of Seattle's low-income households lack emergency kits or plans, and over two-thirds of non-English speakers report no disaster readiness strategy. These gaps create serious vulnerabilities in our community's resilience.

RAVEN directly benefits the community by:

  • Creating a new registered Seattle Emergency Communications Hub that will serve as a critical node in the city's disaster response network. This hub will provide communications capabilities even when traditional infrastructure fails, ensuring that isolated communities maintain connection to vital resources and information.
  • Providing free, accessible workshops on emergency communications, preparedness, and climate resilience that will reach 200+ residents. All activities will be open to the public regardless of background, language, or ability.
  • Distributing over 100 emergency kits to vulnerable households, with priority given to individuals who face barriers to acquiring these resources independently.
  • Establishing weekly community breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park that combine emergency planning with relationship building, creating sustainable connections that transcend emergency situations.

Our project aligns with the Seattle Office of Emergency Management's strategic goals while extending their reach to communities often missed by traditional outreach. By strengthening connections between isolated individuals and established neighborhood structures, we build a more robust citywide emergency response network that leaves no one behind.

Community Involvement

RAVEN is fundamentally community-driven, shaped by collaborative partnerships and designed to engage diverse participants from planning through implementation.

Our core partnerships include:

  • /dev/hack Community Space – Providing indoor workshop space, technical equipment storage, and expertise for radio communications training. Their volunteer tech team will help facilitate radio licensing workshops and support hub communications equipment setup.
  • Cal Anderson Park Alliance – Collaborating on permitting for outdoor community events and supporting public outreach through their established neighborhood networks. Their expertise in park programming will ensure our activities complement existing community uses.
  • Park Punk Pancakes – Co-hosting weekly community breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park, combining their established community food program with our emergency planning activities to create accessible entry points for new participants.

Our community engagement strategy includes multiple pathways for participation:

  • Multi-channel outreach – Utilizing social media, community bulletins, partner networks, and in-person outreach at neighborhood events. We will produce bilingual materials (English/Spanish) to ensure broader accessibility.
  • Regular community presence – Weekly breakfast events provide consistent, low-barrier opportunities for community members to engage with emergency planning in a social context.
  • Skills-based workshops – Offering specific training opportunities that build community capacity while addressing practical emergency needs.
  • Coordination with existing hubs – Regular participation in Seattle Emergency Hub Network meetings to ensure our work complements and enhances citywide efforts.

Community members have shaped this project from its inception, with feedback from diverse stakeholders informing our workshop topics, outreach strategies, and resource distribution priorities.

Volunteer Opportunities

RAVEN offers diverse volunteer roles designed to engage people with varied interests, skills, and availability:

  • Radio Tech Team: Set up and maintain hub communications equipment, assist with radio licensing workshops, and participate in citywide communications drills. Technical experience helpful but not required – training provided.
  • Community Navigators: Lead outreach efforts to vulnerable and underrepresented communities, develop relationships with community organizations, and help connect individuals to appropriate resources.
  • Workshop Facilitators: Prepare and lead training sessions on emergency preparedness, communications basics, and community resilience strategies.
  • Breakfast Brigade: Coordinate weekly community breakfast events at Cal Anderson Park, combining food service with emergency planning activities and relationship building.

Our volunteer management structure ensures sustainable engagement:

  • Dedicated Volunteer Team: Recruits volunteers, matches skills to roles, and provides ongoing support and recognition.
  • Structured onboarding: New volunteers receive orientation to both technical aspects of emergency communications and the community-centered philosophy of our approach.
  • Skills development pathway: Volunteers can develop new capabilities through training opportunities, including FCC licensing support for those interested in radio communications.
  • Regular volunteer gatherings: Monthly volunteer meetings provide opportunities for skill-sharing, feedback, and community building among the volunteer team.

We recognize the diverse knowledge that volunteers bring and create space for both experienced emergency responders and community members new to disaster preparedness. This approach ensures that our hub benefits from both technical expertise and deep community connections.
