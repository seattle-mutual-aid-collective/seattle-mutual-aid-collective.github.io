About Seattle Mutual Aid Collective

The Seattle Mutual Aid Collective is a queer-operated community organization focused on enhancing local resilience efforts and strengthening support networks for vulnerable community members. Our work centers on addressing critical gaps in service provision and creating accessible pathways to resources for communities that face barriers to traditional support systems.

Our committee reflects the diversity of Seattle's communities, with members representing BIPOC, LGBTQ+, immigrant, and disability communities. We recognize the disparities in services and programs reaching these communities and aim to address critical gaps through collaborative initiatives.

Through skill-sharing, resource distribution, and emergency planning, we create connections that empower our communities to respond to crises together, leaving no one behind. The RAVEN Communications Resilience Hub represents our commitment to building tangible infrastructure that serves community needs before, during, and after emergency situations.

Resilience through relationship - We believe that strong communities are the foundation of disaster preparedness, and that relationships built before crisis are essential during emergency response.

Get Involved

The RAVEN project welcomes community participation at all levels. Here are some ways to get involved:

Volunteer - Join our team in roles ranging from workshop facilitation to community outreach to technical communications support. We welcome volunteers with all levels of experience and provide training for those interested in developing new skills.

Attend Workshops - Participate in our emergency preparedness and communications workshops to build your own resilience skills while connecting with community members.

Spread the Word - Help us reach more community members by sharing information about RAVEN with your networks, especially those who might face barriers to traditional emergency preparedness resources.

Connect Us - If you're part of an organization that serves vulnerable communities, we'd love to explore partnership opportunities to extend the reach of our workshops and resources.

To express interest in any of these opportunities, please email us with "RAVEN Involvement" in the subject line.
