Radio Access & Volunteer Emergency Networks

RAVEN (Radio Access & Volunteer Emergency Networks) establishes a Seattle Emergency Communications Hub that builds cross-city resilience networks serving vulnerable populations.

Led by Seattle Mutual Aid Collective with key partners, RAVEN will create resilience pathways by public engagement in radio communications, skills training, and resource sharing:

  • Train 200+ residents through workshops on emergency communications and preparedness
  • Distribute emergency kits to 100+ vulnerable households
  • Integrate with Seattle's Emergency Hub Network while providing specialized communications expertise
  • Co-host weekly community breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park combining emergency planning with relationship building

Seattle faces increasing climate and earthquake risks, yet half of low-income households lack emergency plans. By linking mutual aid networks with citywide infrastructure, RAVEN fosters individual preparation and robust community networks, ensuring no one is left behind.
