RAVEN Project Description

Connecting communities through emergency communications and resilience planning

Project Overview

RAVEN (Radio Access & Volunteer Emergency Networks) establishes a Seattle Emergency Communications Hub that builds cross-city resilience networks serving vulnerable populations. Led by Seattle Mutual Aid Collective with key partners, RAVEN creates resilience pathways through public engagement in radio communications, skills training, and resource sharing.

Seattle faces escalating climate and disaster risks, yet the preparedness gap is substantial: nearly 50% of Seattle's low-income households lack emergency kits or plans, and over two-thirds of non-English speakers report no disaster readiness strategy. These gaps create serious vulnerabilities in our community's resilience.

RAVEN addresses these challenges by creating accessible entry points to emergency preparedness while establishing critical communications infrastructure that will serve the community during crises. Our approach connects existing city emergency systems with communities often left out of traditional preparedness networks.

Registered Emergency Communications Hub
RAVEN will establish a formal Seattle Emergency Communications Hub with specialized radio equipment and trained operators, creating a critical node in the city's disaster response network that ensures isolated communities maintain connection to vital resources and information.
Comprehensive Workshop Series
Our free, accessible workshops will train 200+ residents in emergency communications, first aid, household preparedness, and climate adaptation. All activities are open to the public, with targeted outreach to communities often excluded from traditional emergency planning.
Resource Distribution
RAVEN will distribute over 100 emergency kits to vulnerable households, with priority given to individuals who face barriers to acquiring these resources independently. These kits provide essential supplies for the immediate aftermath of a disaster.
Community Engagement Hub
Weekly community breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park will combine emergency planning with relationship building, creating sustainable connections that transcend emergency situations and provide a consistent, low-barrier opportunity for community engagement.

Community Partnerships

RAVEN is fundamentally community-driven, shaped by collaborative partnerships and designed to engage diverse participants from planning through implementation.

Our core partnerships include:

  • /dev/hack Community Space – Providing indoor workshop space, technical equipment storage, and expertise for radio communications training. Their volunteer tech team will help facilitate radio licensing workshops.
  • Cal Anderson Park Alliance – Collaborating on permitting for outdoor community events and supporting public outreach through their established neighborhood networks.
  • Park Punk Pancakes – Co-hosting weekly community breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park, combining their established community food program with our emergency planning activities.

By partnering with established community organizations, RAVEN leverages existing networks while creating new connections between previously disconnected groups. This approach builds on community strengths while addressing critical gaps in emergency preparedness infrastructure.

Implementation Timeline

RAVEN is fully prepared to launch within 60 days of award notification. We've already assembled a leadership team of 8 members with defined roles, identified 15+ committed volunteers, and secured preliminary partnerships. Five team members already possess FCC Technician licenses or higher, providing the technical foundation for our communications hub.

Phase 1: Commitment & Starter Steps (May 2025)

Finalizing organizational structure, formalizing partnerships, and completing site assessments at Cal Anderson Park and /dev/hack space.

Phase 2: Decision Making & Practice (June 2025)

Procuring equipment, developing communication protocols, completing volunteer recruitment and training, and launching our project website with bilingual resources.

Phase 3: Training & Community Engagement (July-August 2025)

Launching our workshop series, registering with Seattle Emergency Hubs network, conducting communications drills with neighboring hubs, and hosting community events.

Phase 4: Sustainability Planning (September-October 2025)

Evaluating workshop effectiveness, developing long-term volunteer staffing plans, and creating sustainability strategies for ongoing operations beyond the initial funding period.

This phased approach follows the established Hub development model, allowing us to build the project systematically, establish strong community connections, and ensure long-term sustainability.

Project Budget & Community Match

Our budget focuses on practical, direct community benefit through emergency preparedness supplies, communications equipment, and public engagement resources. The total project budget is $15,482.00, allocated entirely to supplies and materials that will directly serve community members.

Key budget elements include:

  • Household Emergency Prep Kits: $7,571.00
  • RAVEN Emergency Communications Hub Equipment: $4,501.00
  • Weekly Community Breakfasts: $1,800.00
  • Shelf-stable Food Supplies: $1,280.00
  • Workshop Materials: $330.00

Community Match Commitment

Our project has secured a strong community match of $9,470.00, representing approximately 61% of our requested budget. This includes:

  • 262 volunteer hours from 12+ community members, valued at $9,170.00
  • $300.00 in cash contributions from individual donors

This substantial community match demonstrates the strong grassroots support for RAVEN and ensures a sustainable foundation for project implementation.

Community Impact

RAVEN integrates with Seattle's Emergency Hub Network while serving communities often excluded from traditional emergency preparedness resources. By linking mutual aid networks with citywide infrastructure, we create multiple pathways for participation while ensuring that emergency preparedness resources reach those who need them most.

Our approach recognizes that effective emergency preparedness requires both individual households with adequate resources and strong community networks that can mobilize quickly during crises. RAVEN addresses both needs by distributing critical supplies while simultaneously building skills and relationships that strengthen community resilience.

By establishing a new Seattle Emergency Communications Hub with particular attention to serving vulnerable populations, RAVEN enhances the city's overall emergency preparedness while ensuring that no community is left behind when disaster strikes.
