Radio Access & Volunteer Emergency Networks

Seattle faces escalating disaster risks—from earthquakes and wildfires to cyberattacks and climate-driven heatwaves—that disproportionately harm marginalized communities. The Seattle Office of Emergency Management’s 2023 Strategic Plan identifies gaps in equitable access to preparedness resources, particularly for LGBTQ+, BIPOC, disabled, and low-income residents. Over 30% of Seattle households lack emergency plans. RAVEN addresses these inequities by building a decentralized, community-led network of emergency communications and mutual aid, ensuring no one is left behind when crises strike.

RAVEN combines hands-on workshops, resource distribution, and volunteer-powered hubs to strengthen neighborhood resilience citywide. Led by LGBTQ+ organizers, this initiative bridges gaps between Seattle’s Emergency Hub Network and vulnerable communities through:


12 Free Skills-Building Workshops
- Emergency Preparedness: 4 workshops to help 100+ residents plan and build emergency kits/go-bags, with free supplies for low-income households.
- Communications Resilience: 3 workshops (Basic Radio Operation, Mesh Networks, Neighborhood Planning) on basic emergency radio operation and coordination with volunteer radio groups. Provide 50 emergency radios to trained community radio operators.
- First-aid training: 2 workshops to train ~24 people on first aid skills (First-Aid, De-escalation training) and supply first aid kits.
- Climate Adaptation and Community Resilience: 1 workshop and weekly Emergency Response Plan Breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park (with Cal Anderson Park Alliance and Park Punk Pancakes) to foster community resiliency planning.


RAVEN Emergency Communications Hub
- Launch a volunteer-led hub registered with Seattle’s Emergency Network
- Establish core team of 5 radio-licensed volunteers
- Redundant communication tools (HAM radios, mesh Wi-Fi, additional licensing requirements).
- A “bridge” team linking isolated individuals to city systems during outages.
- Citywide drill coordinated with cross-district hubs.
- Coordinate planning with Seattle Auxiliary Communications Service (Seattle ACS) and Seattle Emergency Hubs Network.


Resilience Resource Distribution
- Provide 250 preparedness guides with multilingual translations (English/Spanish/Vietnamese).
- Creation of a digital resource directory.


A project initiated by the Seattle Mutual Aid Collective in partnership with /dev/hack Collective, RAVEN integrates with Seattle’s Emergency Hub Network while centering hyperlocal needs. By linking mutual aid with citywide infrastructure, RAVEN builds redundancy and equity in disaster response.
