Implementation Readiness & Work Plan

Project Readiness

RAVEN is fully prepared to launch within 60 days of award notification. We've already completed critical pre-implementation work:

  • Established core partnerships with /dev/hack Collective (secured workspace + technical support), Cal Anderson Park Alliance (venue coordination), and Park Punk Pancakes (community outreach co-hosting)
  • Assembled a leadership team of 8 members with defined roles and relevant expertise
  • Developed workshop themes for emergency communications, radio training and operations, and neighborhood resilience planning
  • Identified 15+ committed volunteers with specific skill sets aligned to project needs
  • 5 team members already possess FCC Technician licenses or higher, with capacity to schedule additional volunteers for licensing exams throughout the project

Our readiness is further evidenced by our phased implementation plan that follows the established Hub development model, beginning with core organization and progressing through training, community engagement, and long-term sustainability.

Work Plan

Our implementation follows four phases based on the Seattle Emergency Hub development model:

Step Activity Responsible Person/Group
Phase 1: Commitment & Starter Steps (May 2025)
1 Finalize Hub organizational structure, confirming roles and responsibilities Project Coordinator, Leadership Committee
2 Formalize partnership agreements with all key partners Project Coordinator, Fiscal Manager
3 Complete site assessments at Cal Anderson Park and /dev/hack space Technology Director, Resource Coordinator
Phase 2: Decision Making & Practice (June 2025)
4 Procure and inventory all equipment and supplies Resource Coordinator, Fiscal Manager
5 Develop communication protocols and emergency response procedures Technology Director, Emergency Planning Coordinator
6 Complete volunteer recruitment and initial training Volunteer Coordinator, Outreach Coordinator
7 Create and launch project website with educational resources in English and Spanish Technology Director, Outreach Coordinator
Phase 3: Training & Community Engagement (July-August 2025)
8 Launch workshop series with community participation Project Coordinator, Emergency Planning Coordinator
9 Register with Seattle Emergency Hubs network Project Coordinator, Technology Director
10 Conduct first communications drill with neighboring hubs Technology Director, Emergency Planning Coordinator
11 Host Climate Adaptation and Food Security workshops Resiliency Lead, Volunteer Coordinator
Phase 4: Sustainability Planning (September-October 2025)
12 Evaluate workshop effectiveness through participant feedback Project Coordinator, Outreach Coordinator
13 Develop long-term volunteer staffing plan Volunteer Coordinator, Project Coordinator
14 Create sustainability and maintenance plan for ongoing operations Resiliency Lead, Leadership Committee

This phased approach allows us to build the project systematically, establish strong community connections, and ensure long-term sustainability. Each phase builds on the previous one, creating a complete emergency communications hub that serves the community while strengthening Seattle's overall emergency preparedness network.
