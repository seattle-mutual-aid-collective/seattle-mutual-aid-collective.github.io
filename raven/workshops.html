Resilience Workshop Series

Building skills, distributing resources, and strengthening community connections through accessible, practical training

Workshop Series Overview

The RAVEN Communications Resilience Hub will host a comprehensive workshop series designed to build practical skills and distribute essential resources to community members. Our workshops focus on four key areas of emergency preparedness and community resilience, creating multiple pathways for participation and skill development.

All workshops are free and open to the public. We conduct targeted outreach to vulnerable and underserved communities who often face challenges accessing standard neighborhood-based resilience efforts, while ensuring all Seattle residents are welcome to participate and build their preparedness skills.

1. Emergency First Aid Training
  • Hands-on first aid training with certified instructors from the Stop-the-Bleed program
  • Essential lifesaving techniques including wound care, bleeding control, and basic injury assessment
  • Neighborhood emergency response coordination and triage basics
  • Distribution of essential first aid supplies to vulnerable individuals and households
These workshops aim to build critical first response capacity within the community, ensuring that neighborhoods have trained individuals who can provide immediate care before professional medical services become available during emergencies.
2. Emergency Preparedness Workshops
  • Emergency kit and go-bag assembly workshops in partnership with Pipsqueak Community Space
  • Household emergency planning guidance, including evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication strategies
  • Home safety assessment techniques and structural vulnerability identification
  • Distribution of emergency supply kits and go-bag supplies to individuals and households with limited resources
Participants will leave with concrete plans, resources, and knowledge to enhance their household preparedness. Our emphasis on accessibility ensures that emergency preparedness is not limited by economic barriers.
3. Communications Resilience Training
  • Radio operations training for emergency and disaster preparedness, including basic FCC licensing preparation
  • Community communications protocols and neighborhood information sharing strategies
  • Hands-on practice with handheld radios and alternative communications methods
  • Coordination with Seattle Emergency Communication Hubs and the Auxiliary Communication Service to practice communications when standard systems fail
These workshops build technical capacity for emergency communications while ensuring participants understand how to integrate with Seattle's established emergency communications networks. Each participant will have the opportunity to practice with equipment that will be made available during actual emergencies.
4. Climate Adaptation and Food Security
  • Scientist-led climate change resilience and adaptation workshops focused on regional impacts
  • Community gardening workshops conducted by SMAC Seed Collective
  • Practical skills for hyperlocal food production and preservation
  • Seed sharing to increase agricultural biodiversity and local food security
Looking beyond immediate disaster response, these workshops build long-term community resilience by addressing underlying vulnerabilities in our food systems and encouraging climate-adaptive practices at the household and neighborhood level.

Each workshop is designed to stand alone while also complementing the others, allowing participants to build a comprehensive set of resilience skills over time. Workshops will be scheduled to accommodate diverse attendance needs, including weekends and evenings, and will be held at accessible locations throughout the project period.

Our weekly community breakfasts at Cal Anderson Park will provide additional opportunities for skills practice, relationship building, and ongoing resilience planning in a more informal setting, creating multiple entry points for community engagement.

All workshop materials and resources will be available in both English and Spanish, with additional language support provided as needed to ensure broad accessibility.
